"Sticks of Fire" is a large, stainless steel 
sculpture in downtown Tampa. It's also 
a mythmaking machine, conjuring  my hometown's 
tireless, optimistic quest to understand how 
it got its name. "Tan-pa" is believed to come 
from pre-Columbian Calusa or Timucuan languages.
According to legend, Tan-pa means "sticks of
fire," but we can't really know if that's true. 

The sculpture serves as both an emblem
of the city's mythmaking apparatus
and as a navigational interface for
this experience. Each of its seven steel 
pillars represents a motif, and when you grab 
a glowing orb beside one of the pillars,
vignettes associated with that motif appear
for you to explore. 

As a nod to Tampa's dubious claim of being 
the lightning capital of North America, 
the music accompanying each motif is generated 
dynamically using lightning data captured 
during local thunderstorms.

You may leave or restart the experience
via a menu accessed by pressing the menu
icon below the left thumbstick.
